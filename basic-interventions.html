Procedure Details

Central Venous Catheter Placement

This fundamental procedure involves placing a catheter into a large vein, typically the subclavian, internal jugular, or femoral vein. The catheter provides reliable access for:

  • Chemotherapy administration
  • Total parenteral nutrition (TPN)
  • Hemodialysis
  • Frequent blood draws
  • Emergency medication access

Recovery: Minimal downtime, patients can resume normal activities within 24-48 hours.

Ultrasound-Guided Biopsy

Real-time ultrasound guidance ensures accurate tissue sampling from any accessible lesion. This technique:

  • Increases diagnostic accuracy to 95%+
  • Reduces patient trauma and complications
  • Provides rapid pathology results
  • Eliminates need for open surgery in many cases

Benefits: Outpatient procedure, returns to normal diet and activity same day.

Nephrostomy Tube Placement

Percutaneous drainage of the kidney for obstruction relief and urine drainage. Essential when:

  • Kidney stones block urine flow
  • Tumors compress the urinary tract
  • Ureter damage requires temporary drainage
  • Sepsis prevention is critical

Outcome: Immediate symptom relief, improved kidney function, can be temporary or long-term as needed.
