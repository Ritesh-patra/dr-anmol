Specialized GI Procedures

Emergency GI Bleeding Embolization

Life-saving intervention for active GI bleeding:

  • Selective catheterization of bleeding vessel
  • Angiographic confirmation of bleeding source
  • Embolization with coils or particles
  • Success rate >95% for hemostasis
  • Lower mortality than surgery in elderly patients
  • Preserves GI continuity

Clinical Note: Angiographic embolization should be first-line treatment for most GI bleeds, often preventing need for surgery.

Colonic Obstruction - Stent Placement

Minimally invasive relief of large bowel obstruction:

  • Fluoroscopic or endoscopic guidance
  • Placement of self-expanding metallic stent
  • Immediate symptom relief (90%+ success)
  • Bridge to surgery in cancer patients
  • Can be definitive treatment in select cases
  • Avoids emergency colostomy

Outcome: Most patients resume normal diet within 24 hours of stent placement.

Percutaneous Gastrostomy (PEG) Placement

Direct feeding access for patients unable to swallow:

  • Image-guided percutaneous approach
  • Can be done under moderate sedation
  • Lasts years with proper care
  • Better than nasogastric tubes for long-term nutrition
  • Lower infection rates than surgical approach
  • Can be easily replaced if dislodged

Candidates: Stroke patients, cancer patients, ALS patients, and others with swallowing dysfunction.

Gastrojejunostomy - Access Beyond the Stomach

Feeding access directly into small bowel for special cases:

  • Used when gastric feeding not tolerated
  • Reduced reflux and aspiration risk
  • Can be combined with gastric decompression
  • Excellent long-term tolerance

Advantage: Allows feeding in patients with severe reflux or delayed gastric emptying.

GI Intervention FAQs

Is angiographic embolization safe? ‚Üì

Yes, embolization is very safe. Complications are rare (<2%) and include temporary vessel damage or bowel ischemia. Benefits far outweigh risks in bleeding emergencies.

How long does a feeding tube last? ‚Üì

With proper care, percutaneous feeding tubes typically last 1-3 years or longer. They can be easily replaced if needed without repeat procedure.

Can colonoscopy stents be used long-term? ‚Üì

Metallic stents can remain long-term, but require monitoring for migration or tissue overgrowth. Regular follow-up endoscopy is recommended.

What's the recovery like after feeding tube placement? ‚Üì

Recovery is minimal. Tube can be used for feeding within 4-6 hours. Patients can be discharged same day or next day with proper care instructions.
