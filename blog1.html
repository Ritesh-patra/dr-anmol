What Are Vascular Blockages & How Are They Treated?

Published on November 26, 2025 | 8 min read

Understanding Vascular Blockages

Vascular blockages, also known as arterial stenosis, occur when fatty deposits or plaque build up inside blood vessels, restricting blood flow. This condition can affect various parts of the body, from the coronary arteries to the peripheral vessels. Early detection is crucial for preventing serious complications.

Common Symptoms

  • Chest pain or pressure during exertion
  • Shortness of breath
  • Numbness or weakness in limbs
  • Intermittent claudication (leg pain while walking)
  • Fatigue and reduced exercise tolerance

Minimally Invasive Treatment Options

Modern endovascular procedures have revolutionized the treatment of vascular blockages. These minimally invasive techniques offer faster recovery times and reduced risk compared to traditional surgery.

Key Treatment Methods:

  • Angioplasty: Widening narrowed arteries with a balloon catheter
  • Stent Placement: Inserting a mesh tube to keep vessels open
  • Atherectomy: Removing plaque buildup from vessel walls
  • Thrombolytic Therapy: Using medication to dissolve blood clots

Recovery & Prevention

Most patients return to normal activities within days after endovascular treatment. Prevention strategies include maintaining a heart-healthy diet, regular exercise, managing stress, and taking prescribed medications. Regular check-ups with your vascular specialist are essential for monitoring your vascular health.
