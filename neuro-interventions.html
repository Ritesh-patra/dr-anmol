Life-Saving Procedures

Acute Ischemic Stroke - Mechanical Thrombectomy

The most time-sensitive neuro intervention. Every minute counts in stroke treatment. Our team:

  • Responds to emergency calls 24/7/365
  • Completes thrombectomy within 60 minutes of arrival
  • Achieves recanalization rates >90% in eligible patients
  • Reduces disability and improves functional outcomes significantly
  • Extended window treatment up to 24 hours in select cases

Clinical Fact:

Mechanical thrombectomy is 3-4 times more effective than clot-busting drugs alone for large vessel occlusions.

Subarachnoid Hemorrhage - Aneurysm Coiling

Emergency treatment for ruptured brain aneurysms with high mortality risk. We provide:

  • Immediate angiography to locate the source
  • Endovascular coiling to prevent re-rupture
  • Advanced coil technologies for complex aneurysms
  • ICU monitoring during critical recovery period
  • Follow-up imaging and surveillance protocols

Clinical Fact:

Endovascular coiling has lower re-rupture rates than traditional surgical clipping with better long-term outcomes.

Arteriovenous Malformations (AVM)

Abnormal blood vessel connections that can cause bleeding and seizures. Treatment options:

  • Endovascular embolization to reduce AVM size
  • Preoperative embolization before surgical resection
  • Multimodal approach combining endovascular and other therapies
  • Careful flow redirection to prevent normal brain damage

Outcome: Reduces bleeding risk and seizure frequency significantly.

Cerebral Stenosis Treatment

Narrowed brain blood vessels that increase stroke risk. We offer:

  • Percutaneous transluminal angioplasty (PTA)
  • Stent placement for vessel support
  • Medical optimization and stroke risk reduction
  • Long-term imaging surveillance

Outcome: Reduces recurrent stroke risk by 60-70%.

Neuro Intervention FAQs

How quickly is mechanical thrombectomy performed? ‚Üì

Our team aims to complete thrombectomy within 60 minutes of hospital arrival in acute stroke. This "door-to-reperfusion" time is critical for patient outcomes.

What is the success rate of aneurysm coiling? ‚Üì

Success rates exceed 90% for aneurysm coiling. Most aneurysms are effectively sealed on first attempt. Re-rupture rates are significantly lower than with surgical clipping.

What are the complications of neuro interventions? ‚Üì

Complications are rare (less than 5%) and include vessel dissection, emboli, or contrast reaction. Our experienced team takes precautions to minimize these risks. Benefits far outweigh risks in emergency situations.

How long is hospital stay after neuro intervention? ‚Üì

Acute stroke thrombectomy patients typically stay 2-5 days for monitoring. Aneurysm coiling patients may need 3-7 days ICU monitoring. Length varies based on individual condition and complications.

Emergency or Consultation?

For emergency stroke or hemorrhage, call 911. For consultations or non-emergency concerns, schedule with Dr Anmol.
